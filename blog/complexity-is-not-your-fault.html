Complexity of the Development Process Is NOT Your Fault

Whether you’re a CEO of a company or an artist, programmer, designer or maybe even a producer working in a game development company, here’s something worth saying out loud: the development process draining you isn’t your personal failure. It’s not a character flaw. It’s not incompetence. It’s the nature of the work.

Every day we navigate a constant stream of decisions. Some tiny, some enormous, many invisible. What to eat? What to prioritize? What to watch? Which task to pick up? What to postpone? And in the middle of all that, we’re asked to make or accept decisions that affect entire teams, schedules, budgets, expectations, and ultimately the player experience.

And often, those decisions don’t come at the right moment. They come too late, or too early, or under pressure, or without enough context. They interrupt flow. They fracture focus. They slowly chip away at mental bandwidth.

Those interruptions leading to shifting expectations, the endless stream of choices or accepting outcomes: That’s what drains you. Not because you’re weak, but because the system is inherently complex.

None of us were built to hold a hundred competing priorities in our heads at once. Yet development asks us to do exactly that, every single day.

It’s not your fault. It’s the cost of navigating complexity without enough support, structure, or clarity.

Deconstruction of fun is easy. Construction of great games is ridiculously hard.

Not because teams aren’t talented, but because modern development is a maze of shifting expectations, endless decisions, unclear responsibilities, and silent misalignment between leadership, teams, and publishers.
